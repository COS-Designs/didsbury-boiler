Persistently dropping pressure usually means there's a small leak somewhere in the system — this could be a valve, a radiator, or a pipe joint. You can top up the pressure yourself in the short term (check your boiler manual for how), but if it keeps dropping you need an engineer. We'll find the source and fix it properly.

A service is a routine annual check — even if your boiler seems fine. A repair is needed when something has gone wrong: error codes, no hot water, no heating, unusual noises, or pressure problems. If you're not sure, just call us and we'll help you work out what you actually need.

There's no separate hidden callout fee. Our repair prices (from £150) include the visit, diagnosis and labour. We'll give you a clear quote before starting any work — what we say it'll cost is what you pay.

Yes — we're fully registered under RK Gas Safe. It's a legal requirement for anyone working on gas appliances in the UK, and we take it seriously. You can verify our registration on the Gas Safe Register at any time.

Most boilers have a reset button — usually marked with a flame or an "R". Press and hold it for 3–10 seconds. If the boiler fires up and stays on, you're done. If it locks out again, there's an underlying fault that needs attention. Don't keep resetting — call us. See our reset guide for more detail.

Our annual service includes: visual inspection of the boiler and controls, checking gas pressure and flow, inspecting the burner and heat exchanger, testing safety devices, checking the flue for obstructions, verifying ventilation, and issuing a Gas Safe certificate. We also flag any issues we spot so you can plan ahead.

Once a year is recommended — ideally before winter. Regular servicing keeps your boiler running efficiently (saving money on bills), extends its lifespan, keeps your warranty valid, and most importantly ensures it's safe. It's a legal requirement for landlords, and highly recommended for homeowners.
